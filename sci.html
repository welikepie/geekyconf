What is GeekyScience?

GeekyScience is an exciting evening of learning and entertainment. Each month, experts in their field will come together to share what they know, related to an overarching scientific theme that will change each month. GeekyScience is curated and hosted by Helen Arney (@helenarney), award-winning comedian and physics graduate.

If you want to learn about the things that live deep within the ocean, how to make an asteroid or what an explosion scientist gets up to day-to-day; GeekyScience is something you donâ€™t want to miss.

October: Ice & Fire

Who invented the firework? How do you make special effects? What on earth is Victorian fire science? What can be done with liquid nitrogen? All these questions and more will be answered at this edition of GeekyScience. Not only that, there'll be some fantastic demonstrations.

Dr. Simon Werrett will be taking us through the history of fireworks, Claire Benson will be demonstrating the world of Victorian fire science, Matthew Tosh will be enlightening us with special effects, Helen Scales will be teaching us about the antarctic cod & antifreeze and Russ Garrett from the London Hackspace will be making something awesome with liquid nitrogen.
